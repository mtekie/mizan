# Mizan Score: Institutional Methodology & Verification Policy
Version: 1.0.0 (Internal Draft)

## 1. Overview
The Mizan Score is a multi-dimensional trust indicator designed for the Ethiopian financial ecosystem. It quantifies "readiness" and "credibility" rather than traditional credit risk alone, bridging the gap for the underbanked.

## 2. The Five Pillars of Trust
| Pillar | Weight | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Profile Completeness** | 30% | Depth of socio-economic data (Employment, Education, Housing). |
| **Savings Consistency** | 20% | Stability of savings patterns and goal achievement. |
| **Budget Discipline** | 20% | Adherence to monthly budget limits and financial planning. |
| **Bill Payment** | 20% | Reliability in meeting recurring obligations (Utilities, Rent). |
| **Verification Level** | 10% | Strength of evidentiary support (Self-declared vs. Linked vs. Photo). |

## 3. Verification Levels
- **Level 1 (Self-Declared)**: User-input data with no third-party corroboration.
- **Level 2 (Linked)**: Data corroborated via automated bank account linking or transactional history.
- **Level 3 (Verified)**: High-assurance data verified via official document photos (Passport, Bank Book, Property Deed).

## 4. Verification Policy
To attain **Level 3 Verification**, users must provide:
1. **Identity**: National ID, Passport, or Digital ID.
2. **Financial Proof**: A physical bank book photo or official stamped statement covering 3+ months.
3. **Asset Proof (Optional)**: Property deeds or business cashflow logs for high-trust product matching.

## 5. Data Privacy & Revocation
Mizan follows a "Zero Knowledge" principle for sharing. Third parties only see the **Score** and the **Verification Badges**. Users can revoke sharing links at any time, instantly invalidating the public URL.
